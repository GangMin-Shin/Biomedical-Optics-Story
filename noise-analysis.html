Noise Analysis

Raw diffuse speckle images are analyzed frame by frame to quantify signal intensity, spatial variance, speckle contrast, dark noise, and shot noise. The analysis compares raw and noise-corrected speckle contrast values for the 15 mm and 20 mm source-detector separation channels.

Analysis Workflow

STEP 01

Raw Image Loading

The raw CSV file is loaded using chunk-based streaming so that long measurements can be processed without loading the entire dataset into memory.

STEP 02

Frame Reconstruction

Every 22 rows are interpreted as one stored frame. The first 21 rows are used to reconstruct the 21 × 21 pixel images, while incomplete remaining rows are excluded.

STEP 03

Channel Separation

Pixel columns 0–20 are assigned to the 15 mm channel, and columns 21–41 are assigned to the 20 mm channel. Each channel therefore contains 441 pixels per frame.

STEP 04

Noise Correction

Measured dark variance, camera specification-based dark variance, and intensity-dependent shot variance are subtracted from the measured spatial variance.

Image and Camera Configuration

Image Region

Image size: 21 × 21 pixels
Pixels per image: 441
Rows per stored frame: 22

Measurement Channels

Channel 1: 15 mm SDS
Channel 2: 20 mm SDS
SDS: Source-Detector Separation

Detector Parameters

Inverse gain: 2.4 e⁻/DN
Exposure time: 2 ms
Quantum efficiency: 0.35

Frame-Level Signal Calculation

For every reconstructed image, all 441 pixels are flattened into one vector. The mean intensity and spatial standard deviation are then used to calculate the raw speckle contrast.

Mean intensity
μI = (1 / N) Σ Ii

N is the number of pixels and Ii is the intensity of the i-th pixel in DN.

Spatial variance
σI2 = [1 / (N − 1)] Σ (Ii − μI)2

The sample variance of the pixel intensities is used to quantify spatial intensity fluctuation within each speckle image.

Raw speckle contrast
Kraw = σI / μI

K is the speckle contrast calculated from the spatial standard deviation divided by the mean pixel intensity.

Raw squared contrast
Kraw2 = σI2 / μI2

The squared speckle contrast is used for variance-based noise subtraction.

Noise Components

Measured dark variance
σdark,measured2 = SD(Idark)2

A separately acquired 128 × 128 background image is used to calculate the measured dark mean, standard deviation, and variance.

Specification dark variance
σdark,spec2 = (24.7 e⁻ / 2.4 e⁻·DN⁻¹)2

The camera specification dark noise is converted from electrons to DN using the inverse system gain.

Shot-noise variance
σshot2 = μI / g

Here, g is the inverse system gain in e⁻/DN. The shot-noise variance therefore changes with the measured mean intensity.

Shot noise
σshot,e⁻ = √(μIg)

The mean signal is converted to electrons before calculating the Poisson shot-noise standard deviation.

Speckle Contrast Correction

Four corrected squared speckle contrast values are generated to compare measured and specification-based dark-noise models, with and without shot-noise subtraction.

Output variable Subtracted noise Calculation
K² measured-dark corrected Measured dark variance max(σ² − σ²dark,measured, 0) / μ²
K² specification-dark corrected Camera specification dark variance max(σ² − σ²dark,spec, 0) / μ²
K² measured-dark and shot corrected Measured dark variance and shot variance max(σ² − σ²dark,measured − σ²shot, 0) / μ²
K² specification-dark and shot corrected Specification dark variance and shot variance max(σ² − σ²dark,spec − σ²shot, 0) / μ²

Important Interpretation Note

Noise subtraction is applied to the variance, not directly to K or K². Negative corrected variances are clipped to zero before division by the squared mean intensity. Therefore, a corrected K² value of zero indicates that the estimated noise variance was equal to or greater than the measured spatial variance under the selected noise model.
