MMF–DSCA Optical Design

MMF–DSCA measures dynamic speckle fluctuation from a tissue sampling volume. The source fiber illuminates tissue, multiply scattered photons pass through the vascular sensitivity region, and the detector fiber bundle transfers speckle patterns to the camera for BFI and BVI calculation.

Microvascular BFI / BVI Simulation

Move the sliders to change red blood cell motion speed and red blood cell amount. Increasing RBC amount slightly expands the vessel, representing a simplified BVI-related volume change.

BFI BFI means Blood Flow Index. In this simplified visualization, faster RBC motion increases temporal speckle fluctuation and therefore represents a higher flow-related index.
BVI BVI means Blood Volume Index. Here, increasing RBC amount increases the apparent blood content and slightly enlarges the vessel cross-section.
Sensitivity volume The yellow banana-shaped region represents the dominant photon sampling volume between source and detector. RBC motion inside this region contributes most strongly to the measured dynamic speckle signal.
