Diffuse Speckle Contrast Analysis (DSCA)

Diffuse Speckle Contrast Analysis (DSCA) is a camera-based diffuse optical flowmetry method. It estimates blood-flow-related dynamics from speckle blurring caused by moving scatterers, mainly red blood cells, and estimates blood-volume-related information from detected intensity changes.

MMF-DSCA Optical Design

1. Single-Longitudinal-Mode Laser

A coherent near-infrared laser provides the illumination needed to form diffuse speckles after multiple scattering in tissue. In the thesis system, a fiber-coupled 808 nm single-longitudinal-mode laser was used as the illumination source.

  • Purpose: generate coherent light so that tissue-scattered optical fields interfere and form speckles.
  • Design point: near-infrared light is preferred because tissue absorption is relatively low compared with visible wavelengths.
  • Practical role: stable optical power and coherence directly affect speckle contrast stability.

1. Speckle Contrast

DSCA measures the blurring of laser speckles during finite camera exposure. Faster red-blood-cell motion causes faster field decorrelation, stronger speckle blurring, and lower speckle contrast.

K = σI / <I>
K² = σI² / <I>²

2. Blood Flow Index

BFI is interpreted as a blood-flow-related optical index derived from speckle contrast. In the practical relative-flow regime, lower K² corresponds to higher flow-related dynamics.

BFIrel ∝ 1 / K²

3. Blood Volume Index

BVI is derived from the total detected intensity. Because blood absorption changes the detected photon intensity, BVI gives complementary blood-volume-related information.

BVI = −log(Σ Ipixels)

Detailed Component Summary

Component Optical / Signal Role Implementation Detail
SLM Laser Generates coherent near-infrared illumination for diffuse speckle formation. SLM means single-longitudinal-mode, which improves temporal coherence. Thesis system: fiber-coupled 808 nm CrystaLaser, nominal 100 mW. Your parts list screenshot also includes DL785-120-SO for the 785 nm version.
Fiber Beam Splitters Divide one laser output into multiple source channels for multi-source diffuse optical sampling. Cascaded 50:50 multimode fiber optic couplers produce four source paths. Each channel can be balanced using attenuation or alignment control.
Source Fibers Deliver light to tissue at defined source positions. The source-detector separation determines the depth-weighted sensitivity profile. Multimode source fibers with 200 μm core and 0.22 NA are consistent with the listed Thorlabs patch cables.
Tissue Sampling Volume Multiple scattering creates a diffuse photon path distribution. Moving red blood cells modulate the temporal speckle statistics. DSCA does not image vessels directly; it measures flow-sensitive speckle statistics integrated over the photon sampling volume.
1×7 Fan-Out MMF Bundle Collects diffusely reflected speckles from multiple detection channels and transmits them to the camera. Thesis system: BF72HS01, 1-to-7 fan-out bundle, 200 μm-core multimode fibers.
Relay Lens Assembly Images the fiber-output speckle spots onto the camera sensor while preserving separable channel ROIs. A 1:1 relay lens assembly was used. Your parts list includes LA1951-B, a plano-convex lens with AR coating for 650–1050 nm.
CMOS Camera Captures spatial speckle images for contrast calculation. Multi-channel acquisition is possible because several fiber outputs can be imaged simultaneously. Thesis system: Basler acA1300-60gm, 1282 × 1026 pixels, 5.3 μm pixel size, 60 fps specification. Your camera experiments may use higher effective frame settings depending on ROI and camera configuration.
ROI Processing Calculates BFI from speckle contrast and BVI from integrated intensity. A channel ROI is selected over the speckle spot; sub-ROI averaging improves stability by spatial ensemble averaging.
